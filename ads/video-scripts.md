# Video ad scripts and production options

_June 2026. Companion to the image templates in this folder._

## The honest options, cheapest-effective first

1. **David to camera, filmed on a phone (recommended first).** For founder-led
   consultancy, an unpolished founder beats a polished animation — buyers are
   hiring people, and authenticity is the differentiator AI-generated video
   can't fake. One afternoon, natural light, a tidy background, the scripts
   below. The existing about-video.mp4 proves the format already works for us.
2. **AI avatar tools (Synthesia, HeyGen).** Type a script, get a presenter
   video. Acceptable for retargeting filler; risky as a first impression for a
   company selling AI judgement — savvy buyers clock it, and "they used a fake
   person" is a bad story for us specifically.
3. **Google's auto-generated video.** Demand Gen and PMax campaigns can
   assemble video from our images and text automatically. Free, mediocre,
   fine as a by-product — never as the plan.
4. **Generative video (Veo, Sora, Runway).** Impressive for product/brand
   spots; wrong for a trust-led services firm at our budget. Revisit when
   there's a real production budget and a creative reason.

Rule of thumb: spend the video budget on (1), use (3) as free coverage, skip
(2) and (4) for now.

## Filming notes

- Phone at eye level, landscape 1080p minimum; film each script twice.
- Natural light from the front, not behind. Quiet room beats clip-on mic fuss.
- First sentence within two seconds — YouTube skips at five.
- Captions on (most feed video plays muted); CapCut auto-captions are fine.
- Export 16:9 for YouTube, 1:1 and 9:16 crops for social reuse.
