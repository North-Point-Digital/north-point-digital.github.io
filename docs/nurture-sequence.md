_Five emails over ~16 days for people who download the AI Adoption Playbook.
Written to be sent manually from David's address (recommended at current
volume — personalise the first line where you can), or loaded into a free
automation tool (MailerLite/Brevo) when volume justifies it. Plain text, no
templates, no banners: the more it looks like a normal email, the better it
performs._

## Email 2 — day 3
**Subject: The question that kills most AI projects**

It isn't "which AI should we use?" It's "what exactly should it do?"

The projects that stall almost always started with a technology looking for
a job. The ones that work start with a job: a task someone does every week,
that follows roughly the same steps, where the information already exists
somewhere in your business.

Quick test you can do over a coffee — ask yourself:
- What does my team retype, re-answer, or re-look-up every single week?
- If that took ten minutes instead of three hours, what would it be worth a year?

That's your candidate use case. If you've got one in mind, hit reply and
tell me what it is — happy to give you an honest read on whether AI can do
it, no charge, no call required.

David

## Operating notes

- **Now (manual):** twice a week, check new playbook contacts in HubSpot and
  send the next email due. Use a text expander or saved drafts. Personalise
  one line of email 1 when the company is identifiable.
- **Replies beat clicks.** Emails 1 and 2 ask questions deliberately — a
  reply moves someone from "lead" to "conversation", which is the actual
  goal. Answer fast.
- **Later (automated):** load these into MailerLite or Brevo (free tiers
  include automation) when downloads pass ~30/month. Keep plain text.
- **Update before sending:** founding-slot copy in email 5 must match the
  live site — when slots are taken, retire or amend it.
- Sign-off, claims and tone follow the site rules (docs/redesign-spec.md):
  UK English, concrete, no hype.
